Relationship between Poverty rate and Coronary heart conditions in the US

The plot above shows the correlation between the % of population living below the poverty line and the heart condition in the US . As shown in the chart, there seems to be a clear positive relationship between the two variables ; As poverty level increases the heart conditions tend to deteriorate . Further, while most 50 states are clustered within a poverty rate of 10-20%, Puerto Rico appears to be an outlier with a poverty rate little less than 50% and with the highest level of heart condition deterioration felt by surveyed individuals
